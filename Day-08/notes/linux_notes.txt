# Day 8 - Combining Commands with Pipe

## Commands Learned

- head
- tail
- wc -l
- wc -w
- wc -c
- |

## Mistakes I Made

- I typed `wc -w5` instead of `wc -w`.
- I typed `wc -l6` instead of `wc -l`.
- I typed `wc -c49` instead of `wc -c`.

## How I Fixed Them

- I learned that `wc` options do not take numbers after them.
- The number should be used with `head` or `tail`, not wit
 `wc`.
- I corrected the commands and used the proper syntax.

## What I Learned

- `head` and `tail` show only part of a file.
- `wc` counts lines, words, or bytes.
- The pipe `|` sends the output of one command to
another command.
- Combining commands makes Linux more powerful.

## My Observation

At first, I mixed the options of `head` and `wc`.
Now I understand that `head` uses numbers to
choose how many lines to show, while `wc`
only counts the output.

## Real-world Use

Linux administrators and DevOps engineers combine
commands with pipes to analyze log files, count data,
and process information quickly without opening
the entire file.

## Challenge Completed

I created a file with 15 lines, viewed only the required lines,
counted the lines, words, and bytes, and practiced
combining `head` with `wc` using a pipe.
