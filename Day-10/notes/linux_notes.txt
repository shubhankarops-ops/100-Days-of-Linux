# Day 10 - Sorting and Removing Duplicate Lines

## Commands Learned

- sort
- uniq
- sort | uniq

## Mistakes I Made

- I typed `READMR.md` instead of `README.md`.
- I typed `linux_noyes.txt` instead of `linux_notes.txt`.
- I typed `filrs` instead of `files`.
- I forgot to add the `.txt` extension to the file name.
- I thought `sort` finds duplicate lines.
- I thought `uniq` works like `cat`.

## How I Fixed Them

- I corrected the spelling mistakes.
- I learned that `sort` only arranges lines in
 alphabetical order.
- I understood that `uniq` removes only consecutive
 duplicate lines.
- I learned that `sort | uniq` is the correct way to
remove duplicate lines from an unsorted file.

## What I Learned

- `sort` arranges lines alphabetically.
- `uniq` removes consecutive duplicate lines.
- `sort | uniq` first sorts the file and then
removes duplicate lines.
- The order of commands in a pipe is important.

## My Observation

At first, I thought `sort` searched for duplicate lines
and `uniq` worked like `cat`.
After practicing, I understood that `sort`
organizes the data, while `uniq`
removes repeated consecutive lines.

## Real-world Use

System Administrators and DevOps Engineers
use `sort` and `uniq` to organize large log files,
remove duplicate entries, and prepare clean
reports for analysis.

## Challenge Completed

I practiced `sort`, `uniq`, and `sort | uniq`
and understood why the order of commands is important.
