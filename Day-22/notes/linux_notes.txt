Linux Notes - Day 22

1. cp = Copy files or folders.

2. cp file1 file2
   Copies one file.

3. cp -r folder backup
   Copies a complete folder.

4. Use cp when you want to keep the original file.

5. Use mv when you want to move or rename a file.

6. tee
   Displays output on screen and saves it to a file.

Example:

echo "Hello Linux" | tee report.txt

7. tee -a
   Appends text without deleting old content.

8. sort | uniq
   Shows only unique values from sorted data.

9. find . -name "*.log"
   Finds all log files.

10. history
    Shows previously executed commands.
